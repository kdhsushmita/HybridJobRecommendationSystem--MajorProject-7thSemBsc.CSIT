Finding Job is a hassle in Nepal, and here’s how you can make it easier

Date: 14 Oct - Time: 4:36 pm - Author: chawanprash
Skills

There is nothing new in the sentence, “It is hard to find jobs in Nepal,” and I do not have to prove it. If you are searching for a job vacancy here in Nepal, you know it. But the thing is, many companies are hiring teams and not finding one. It is not that there are no employment opportunities; it is that the hirers are not finding a perfect fit for the company, and the seekers aren’t able to grab such opportunities.

Job Vacancy: Why are people not finding jobs in Nepal?

Finding opportunities in Nepal can be a daunting task. The employment landscape in the country poses several challenges for job seekers and employers alike. In this article, we will explore some of the key factors contributing to the difficulty of securing a job in Nepal.

High Vacancy Requirements

As I was searching through LinkedIn for a job one day, I only found vacancies with at least 4 years of experience in a particular field. You are required to complete a bachelor’s or master’s degree to apply for that job. These requirements would have been okay for senior-level posts; however, the vacancies were for junior software engineers. Plus, there are not a whole lot of opportunities for freshmen or entry-level human resources.

Top jobs on LinkedIn when I search for Junior Posts

I mean, some jobs might require an individual to complete a course. Nevertheless, the way opportunities are posted discourages potential candidates from applying, especially in sectors like IT, where skills should outweigh formal education. This is the reason I hesitated to apply for any job vacancy.

What amused me were the experience boundaries for entry-level positions in the context of Nepal. By definition, an entry-level job is “typically one you can perform without any prior education or experience because you will likely receive some amount of on-the-job training to be successful.” (Coursera)  However, as it seems, you need experience to even start a career here in Nepal. Internships are the only way to enter an industry, which brings me to the next point!

Internship scam

Internships can be valuable for gaining experience. All require you to work for some time before they officially hire you or throw you out. Probably fine, but many here operate in a way that may not be beneficial for job seekers. Most of the internships in Nepal are pure bullshit.

When you hear that you will be working for 3+ months for free after your first interview, what will be your reaction? Even though the whole purpose of an internship is to learn, the interns need a way to incentivize their workflow. This creates an economic barrier for many individuals and discourages them from taking on internships, despite the promise of experience.

Skill Exposure

I am only highlighting, or, should I say, ranting, about the employer’s fault. Sometimes, you (applicants) are also ‘faulty’ here. One prime mistake from ya’ all is the way you expose your skill.

Hear me out: “I can do anything for the company” doesn’t align with the expertise any company is looking for. They need a specific set of skills for their job to be done. Hence, to stand out in the competitive job market in Nepal, applicants should focus on highlighting their expertise in a particular area rather than adopting a one-size-fits-all approach.

So, how can I find one?

Finding yourself a job is not easy. You have to dwell in the depths of the internet to find one. Finding a job online is the way to go right now in Nepal. Here are some of the tips I would give:

Learn relevant skills

Without learning the damn skills required, do not even think of applying for the vacancy. I see most people around my circle trying to apply for jobs, forcing every bit of knowledge within a week or even a day before the interview. This is not how the interview process works in Nepal (or, for that matter, any other place)! Even if it is for an internship, the hirer will not pick you until they see your potential to work in that field.

I suggest getting some free online courses. The paid ones (online or offline) are not as effective, and you might get lazy and quit them anyway. So, start with a quick course or tutorial on video platforms like YouTube and read books or articles about it. Heck! When you are committed to the chosen career path, you might as well dive into some paid courses afterward.

Create a Well-Managed CV

A CV is the first thing the company will see before even knowing about you. So, rather make it your top priority to maintain the CV. Highlight your skills, education, and experience with it. Here’s the format of the CV to begin with.

Creative Blue Resume

Use tools and templates

If you are too lazy or incapable of creating your own damn resume, you can always use a resume builder website like Resume.io or work your way up from a template. Whatever you choose to do must be clean and to the point. That’s how you grab the opportunities.

Online Applications

There are many job openings for Nepal lying around online out there. Sign up for those websites and commit yourself to applying. Apply for more than one job at a time, even if the job does require a higher level of experience (only if you have the skill). These will probably get you a position in the company.

Here are some of the websites you can try:

LinkedIn

Yes, at the top of my list is LinkedIn; no big surprise. As it is the best social networking site for seeking a vacancy worldwide, many Nepali companies are registered and post opportunities every day. You will likely find jobs related to your career. The further advantage of this website is that it is international, so there are remote vacancies you can work on without leaving Nepal as well.

Some of the other options are:

  • Merojob
  • Merorojgari
  • Hamrojob
  • Jobjee
  • JobsNepal
  • Kumari Job

Social Media

Social media sites like Facebook, Instagram, etc. have a lot of ads for openings in Nepal. If you search for a job online for a while, your data will be sold online, and you will find a ton of ads asking for similar jobs. You also have Twitter (currently named X by Musk), Reddit (but it’s dangerous out there), and TikTok! I can’t think of any other platforms, but the point I’m trying to make is that these will make your life easier.

Connections

And I almost forgot the main point—connection. No one will know you better than your close ones. They will know your attitude towards the field. Hence, it is much easier to get a job if you have a broader connection. So, widen your network and try finding a placement within it. This might also save you the hassle of applications, interviews, and such.

Biases

One thing I should probably mention here is that bias also plays a vital role. Hence, if you are applying for a post in a company with no connection whatsoever, the probability is that the person closer to the company is preferred over you. It’s not that you aren’t in the hiring equation; it’s just that someone else will be getting more priority. Hence, the problem Network > Skill arises in some way or another.

Some of my other tips

Here are some of my other tips. (Note: not applicable to everyone.)

Better cover letter

Other than a CV, your only way to communicate with the hirer is through a cover letter. Hence, tailor your cover letter to explain why you are a better choice for the company rather than repeating everything from your CV.

Apply for remote/online job

Keeping you limited within a region limits the opportunity. Hence, applying for an online job far from your city or even outside Nepal increases your chances of getting a job. Not only is the pay better, but you will also better understand the sector in which you are trying to make a living.

Freelancing

Freelancing is one of the ways to create a strong portfolio and, side-by-side, gain experience. Start with a cheaper fee, then work your way up. You will get good at the service while earning some cash (unlike those internships). And the best part is that these would also add to your CV.

Job Vacancy in Nepal: Conclusion

Finding job vacancies in Nepal can become a pain due to various factors, including high job requirements, experience barriers, internship issues, or a lack of skill exposure. However, improving your skills, maintaining a well-structured CV, and utilizing online job portals can enhance your prospects in the current competitive market. Moreover, having a network can save you a ton of hassle if you have that potential!
